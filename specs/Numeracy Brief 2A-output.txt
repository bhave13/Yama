## 3. Skill Levels

*Note: As per user specifications, standard atomisation rules are suspended for this capstone topic. These "levels" represent rich, comparative contexts requiring comprehensive PO3 justifications using the PEEL format.*

---

### Context: Hospitality

**Tier:** Context Problem
**Single Skill Tested:** Evaluates two catering or recipe options involving mass, capacity, and cost, justifying a recommendation using the PEEL structure and comparative numerical evidence.
**Recommended Question Types:** EXPLANATION

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Whole numbers for mass/capacity and straightforward serving multipliers. | Comparing two recipes where Recipe A makes 10 servings and Recipe B makes 20 servings. |
| 2 | Decimal multipliers or decimal costs per serving. | Comparing ingredient costs that total $14.50 vs $12.75. |
| 3 | Fractional scaling or combining mixed metric units. | Scaling a recipe up by 1.5 times, requiring conversion from mL to L. |
| 4 | Multi-step calculations involving wastage, fractional scaling, and mixed metric conversions. | Comparing bulk buying options where 15% of the produce is unusable. |

**Example Question Stem:** "You are catering a school camp for 80 students. Supplier A sells 5L bottles of juice for $12, while Supplier B sells 750mL bottles in packs of 6 for $10. Which supplier is the most cost-effective option to ensure every student gets a 250mL serving? State your position and explain your choice using numbers from both options."

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student states a position but provides no numerical calculations to back it up (fails to provide "Evidence").
- Student calculates the cost for one supplier but ignores the other, failing to make a true comparison.
- Student forgets to standardize the units (e.g., comparing liters directly to milliliters without conversion).

---

### Context: Woodwork

**Tier:** Context Problem
**Single Skill Tested:** Evaluates two building plans or material supply options involving area, volume, and metric length conversions, justifying a choice using the PEEL structure and comparative numerical evidence.
**Recommended Question Types:** EXPLANATION

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Whole number side lengths in the same metric unit. | Comparing two rectangular planter boxes measured in meters. |
| 2 | Decimal side lengths or simple single-step conversions. | Calculating area where lengths are given as 2.4m and 1.2m. |
| 3 | Mismatched metric units requiring conversion before calculation. | Comparing a board that is 2000mm long with one that is 1.8m long. |
| 4 | Multi-step compound volume calculations or calculating minimizing wastage off a larger sheet. | Comparing the yield of cut pieces from two differently sized standard plywood sheets. |

**Example Question Stem:** "You are building raised garden beds. Design 1 requires a volume of soil measuring 2m by 1m by 0.5m. Design 2 requires a volume measuring 150cm by 120cm by 60cm. Soil costs $40 per cubic meter. Which design is cheaper to fill, and by how much? Write a PEEL paragraph to justify your answer, referring to both designs."

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student multiplies mixed units (e.g., $150 \times 1.2 \times 60$) without converting them to a common unit first.
- Student calculates volume correctly but forgets the final step of calculating and comparing the cost.

---

### Context: Digital Technology

**Tier:** Context Problem
**Single Skill Tested:** Evaluates technology scenarios involving 24-hour time, duration, and data capacity, justifying a schedule or plan using the PEEL structure and comparative numerical evidence.
**Recommended Question Types:** EXPLANATION

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Whole hour elapsed times and integer data capacities. | Comparing a 2-hour render time vs a 4-hour render time. |
| 2 | Durations involving 30-minute intervals and decimal file sizes. | Comparing a backup that takes 1.5 hours vs 45 minutes. |
| 3 | Calculations crossing the hour mark or converting minutes to fractional hours. | Comparing a process starting at 09:45 and ending at 11:20. |
| 4 | Complex scheduling involving multiple sequential tasks and 24-hour time across midnight. | Comparing two overnight server backup schedules that must finish before 06:00. |

**Example Question Stem:** "Server Backup A takes 145 minutes and must start after 22:30. Server Backup B takes 2 hours and 15 minutes but can only start at 23:00. The system needs to be back online by 01:00. Which backup option meets the deadline? Use a PEEL paragraph to explain your answer, showing the finish times for both options."

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student treats time as a base-10 decimal system (e.g., treating 145 minutes as 1.45 hours).
- Student incorrectly calculates time elapsed across midnight.

---

### Context: Money Matters

**Tier:** Context Problem
**Single Skill Tested:** Evaluates business performance or staff scheduling using statistical charts, min/max, and range, justifying a business decision using the PEEL structure and comparative numerical evidence.
**Recommended Question Types:** EXPLANATION

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Extracting whole number integers from simple bar charts or tables. | Comparing weekly sales totals of $500 vs $600. |
| 2 | Extracting decimal values (currency) from tables and calculating simple ranges. | Comparing the highest and lowest daily profit from a provided table. |
| 3 | Reading grouped data charts or calculating hourly wages with fractional hours. | Comparing two staff rosters where shifts are 4.5 hours long. |
| 4 | Synthesizing data from multiple visual sources (e.g., a table and a line graph) to make a compound decision. | Evaluating whether Business A's consistent low range is better than Business B's high maximum but massive range. |

**Example Question Stem:** "Look at the sales graphs for Cafe A and Cafe B over a 7-day period. Cafe A has a maximum daily sale of $1200 but a minimum of $200. Cafe B's daily sales range between $750 and $900. If you were looking to buy a business with reliable, steady income, which cafe would you choose? Support your decision with numbers from both graphs and compare their ranges."

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student misreads the scale on the axes of the provided charts.
- Student references only the maximum value (the "peak") and ignores the range/consistency when evaluating reliability.
