# YAMA Topic Analysis

## Topic: Metric Unit Conversions
## Year Group: Year 9
## Curriculum Strand: Geometry and Measurement
## Achievement Objective: Select and use appropriate metric units for length, volume and capacity, and weight (mass); convert between metric units using whole number and decimal multipliers (NZ Curriculum Level 5)

---

## 1. Prerequisites

Prerequisites are skills the student must have consolidated before beginning this topic.
Each row is a single, independently learnable skill, ordered from most fundamental.

| # | Prerequisite Skill | Why It Is Needed |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Multiply whole numbers by 10, 100, and 1000 | Converting from a larger unit to a smaller unit requires multiplication by a power of 10 |
| 2 | Divide whole numbers by 10, 100, and 1000 | Converting from a smaller unit to a larger unit requires division by a power of 10 |
| 3 | Multiply and divide decimal numbers by 10, 100, and 1000 | Many measurement values (e.g. 1.75 kg, 2.3 km) are given as decimals; students must move the decimal point correctly |
| 4 | Recognise powers of 10 up to 10^6 and express large multipliers in standard form | Mass conversions span six powers of 10 (µg to Mt); students need to handle very large and very small multipliers |
| 5 | Read and write common fractions and convert between fractions and decimals | Some conversions produce fractional results (e.g. 500 m = ½ km); students must express and interpret these correctly |

## 3. Skill Levels

ATOMISATION RULE: Each level tests exactly ONE new, independently assessable skill.
Level count is determined by the topic — use as many as needed.

---

### Level 1: Multiply and Divide by Powers of Ten

**Tier:** Pre-req Check
**Single Skill Tested:** Multiply or divide a given number by 10, 100, or 1000
**Recommended Question Types:** NUMERIC, MCQ

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Multiply or divide a whole number by 10, 100, or 1000 | 45 × 1000 = ? |
| 2 | Multiply or divide a decimal by 10, 100, or 1000 | 3.72 × 100 = ? |
| 3 | Multiply or divide a fraction by 10, 100, or 1000 | ¾ × 1000 = ? |
| 4 | Multiply or divide by 10 000 or 1 000 000, or divide to produce a small decimal | 0.0075 × 1 000 000 = ? |

**Example Question Stem:** 240 × 1000 = ?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student adds zeros without adjusting the decimal point (e.g. writes 3.7 × 100 = 3.700 rather than 370)
- Student moves the decimal in the wrong direction when dividing (e.g. 4500 ÷ 1000 = 45 000 instead of 4.5)

---

### Level 2: Length Conversion Facts

**Tier:** Facts
**Single Skill Tested:** Recall and state the conversion factor between any two adjacent length units (km, m, cm, mm)
**Recommended Question Types:** MCQ, MATCH, TEXT

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | State how many of the smaller unit equal one of the larger unit (whole number answer) | How many centimetres are in one metre? |
| 2 | State the factor for a less familiar adjacent pair | How many millimetres are in one centimetre? |
| 3 | Express the conversion as a unit fraction going upward | 1 mm = 1/? m |
| 4 | State the combined factor between non-adjacent units (derived from the chain) | How many millimetres are in one kilometre? |

**Example Question Stem:** How many metres are in one kilometre?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student confuses 1 m = 100 cm with 1 m = 1000 mm, mixing up the 100 and 1000 factors
- Student applies the m → cm factor (100) to the cm → mm step, believing 1 cm = 100 mm

---

### Level 3: Mass Conversion Facts

**Tier:** Facts
**Single Skill Tested:** Recall and state the conversion factor between any two adjacent mass units (Mt, t, kg, g, mg, µg)
**Recommended Question Types:** MCQ, MATCH, TEXT

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | State the conversion for the most familiar adjacent pair (kg ↔ g) | How many grams are in one kilogram? |
| 2 | State the conversion for less familiar pairs (g ↔ mg, or t ↔ kg) | How many milligrams are in one gram? |
| 3 | State the conversion as a unit fraction going upward | 1 g = 1/? kg |
| 4 | Name the unit given its position in the chain relative to the base unit g | Which unit is 1000 times bigger than a gram and 1000 times smaller than a tonne? |

**Example Question Stem:** How many grams are in one kilogram?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student uses a factor of 100 instead of 1000 for mass conversions (confusion with cm → m)
- Student assumes the factor varies across steps (e.g. thinks t → kg uses 100 and kg → g uses 1000)
- Student confuses milligram (mg) and microgram (µg), or does not know the symbol µ

---

### Level 4: Volume and Capacity Conversion Facts

**Tier:** Facts
**Single Skill Tested:** Recall and state the conversion factors for volume and capacity, including the cm^3 = mL equivalence
**Recommended Question Types:** MCQ, MATCH, TEXT

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | State the most common capacity conversion (L ↔ mL) | How many millilitres are in one litre? |
| 2 | State the kilolitre conversion (kL ↔ L) | How many litres are in one kilolitre? |
| 3 | State the volume–capacity bridge (cm^3 = mL) | One cubic centimetre holds how many millilitres? |
| 4 | State the derived relationship between m^3 and L | How many litres are in one cubic metre? |

**Example Question Stem:** How many millilitres are in one litre?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student believes 1 L = 100 mL (borrowing the m → cm factor of 100)
- Student does not recognise that cm^3 and mL are equivalent, treating them as unrelated units
- Student cannot recall whether 1 m^3 = 100 L or 1000 L

---

### Level 5: Time Conversion Facts

**Tier:** Facts
**Single Skill Tested:** Recall and state the conversion factors between seconds, minutes, hours, and days
**Recommended Question Types:** MCQ, MATCH

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | State the conversion between the most familiar adjacent pair (min ↔ sec or hr ↔ min) | How many seconds are in one minute? |
| 2 | State the day–hour conversion | How many hours are in one day? |
| 3 | State a derived non-adjacent conversion factor | How many seconds are in one hour? |
| 4 | State the full chain conversion (day → sec) | How many seconds are in one day? |

**Example Question Stem:** How many minutes are in one hour?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student applies the metric ×1000 rule to time (e.g. believes 1 hour = 1000 minutes)
- Student confuses base-60 counting (time) with base-10 counting (metric), producing incorrect factors

---

### Level 6: Convert from a Larger Unit to a Smaller Unit (Single Step)

**Tier:** Simple Skill
**Single Skill Tested:** Convert a measurement from a larger unit to its adjacent smaller unit by multiplying by the correct conversion factor
**Recommended Question Types:** NUMERIC, SPOT_ERROR/VALUE

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Whole number input, whole number output | 5 km = ? m |
| 2 | Decimal input, simple decimal or whole number output | 2.4 m = ? cm |
| 3 | Fractional input | ¾ kg = ? g |
| 4 | Values producing very large outputs or involving less familiar units (Mt → t, mg → µg) | 0.003 Mt = ? t |

**Example Question Stem:** A length of timber is 3 m. How many centimetres is this?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student divides instead of multiplies, converting in the wrong direction
- Student uses the wrong conversion factor (e.g. multiplies by 100 when converting km → m instead of 1000)
- Student places the decimal point incorrectly when the input is a decimal (e.g. 2.4 m = 24 cm instead of 240 cm)

---

### Level 7: Convert from a Smaller Unit to a Larger Unit (Single Step)

**Tier:** Simple Skill
**Single Skill Tested:** Convert a measurement from a smaller unit to its adjacent larger unit by dividing by the correct conversion factor
**Recommended Question Types:** NUMERIC, SPOT_ERROR/VALUE

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Input exactly divisible, giving a whole number result | 3000 m = ? km |
| 2 | Division gives a simple decimal result | 250 cm = ? m |
| 3 | Division gives a result best expressed as a fraction | 750 mL = ? L |
| 4 | Very small values or less familiar units | 500 µg = ? mg |

**Example Question Stem:** A bottle contains 2000 mL. How many litres is this?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student multiplies instead of divides, converting in the wrong direction
- Student writes the decimal point in the wrong position (e.g. 250 cm = 25.0 m instead of 2.5 m)
- Student is uncertain whether to multiply or divide and guesses, lacking a decision strategy

---

### Level 8: Apply the cm^3 = mL Volume–Capacity Equivalence

**Tier:** Simple Skill
**Single Skill Tested:** Convert between volume expressed in cm^3 and capacity expressed in mL or L using the equivalence 1 cm^3 = 1 mL
**Recommended Question Types:** NUMERIC, MCQ, EXPLANATION

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Convert whole number cm^3 directly to mL | 350 cm^3 = ? mL |
| 2 | Convert cm^3 to L using the chain: cm^3 → mL → L | 2500 cm^3 = ? L |
| 3 | Convert in the reverse direction: L or mL → cm^3 | 0.75 L = ? cm^3 |
| 4 | Extend to m^3 ↔ kL, or chain across three unit changes | 1 500 000 cm^3 = ? kL |

**Example Question Stem:** A container holds 500 cm^3 of water. How many millilitres is this?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student does not recognise cm^3 and mL as equivalent, inventing a separate conversion factor
- Student confuses cm^3 (a unit of volume) with cm (a unit of length), thinking "cubic" means "multiply by 3"
- Student converts cm^3 to mL correctly but fails to apply the subsequent mL → L step

---

### Level 9: Convert Between Adjacent Time Units (Single Step)

**Tier:** Simple Skill
**Single Skill Tested:** Convert a time measurement between two adjacent units by applying the correct non-decimal conversion factor (×60, ×24, ÷60, or ÷24)
**Recommended Question Types:** NUMERIC, SPOT_ERROR/VALUE, MCQ

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Multiply or divide by 60 or 24 with a whole number input giving a whole number result | 3 hours = ? minutes |
| 2 | Divide to produce a simple decimal result | 90 seconds = ? minutes |
| 3 | Multiply or divide where the result requires careful handling of the non-decimal factor | 2.5 hours = ? seconds (via minutes) |
| 4 | Convert between non-adjacent time units in a single calculated step using a known derived factor | 1.5 days = ? minutes (using 1 day = 1440 min) |

**Example Question Stem:** A YouTube video is 150 minutes long. How many hours is this?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student applies the metric ×1000 rule to time (e.g. writes 2 hours = 2000 minutes)
- Student divides when converting from hours to minutes, or multiplies when converting from minutes to hours (wrong direction)
- Student uses ×100 for the hour → minute step by analogy with m → cm

---

### Level 10: Challenge — Mixed Multi-Step Conversions

**Tier:** Complex Skill
**Single Skill Tested:** Select and execute the correct multi-step conversion strategy for a problem drawn from any metric or time measurement domain, without being told which domain or how many steps are required
**Recommended Question Types:** NUMERIC, SPOT_ERROR/STEP, SPOT_ERROR/VALUE, EXPLANATION, MCQ

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Two-step chain, whole number values, single domain (length or mass) | 4 km = ? cm  (km → m → cm) |
| 2 | Two-step chain with decimal values, OR mixed time units to a single unit | 1.5 t = ? g  OR  2 hours 30 minutes = ? minutes |
| 3 | Three-step metric chain, OR time across three units | 2 km = ? mm  OR  1 day 6 hours 20 minutes = ? minutes |
| 4 | Long chain (4+ steps) across mass or time, or questions spanning two different domains in the same set | 0.002 Mt = ? mg  OR  2 days 3 hours 45 minutes 30 seconds = ? seconds |

**Example Question Stem:** A road is 3 km long. Express this distance in millimetres.

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student stops after one conversion step and does not continue the chain
- Student applies the metric ×1000 rule to time conversions (e.g. 1 hour = 1000 minutes)
- Student uses an incorrect intermediate unit when chaining (e.g. jumps directly from km to mm, applying the wrong combined factor)
- Student sequences the correct individual factors in the wrong order

---

### Level 11: Real-World Conversion Problems (NZ Context)

**Tier:** Context Problem
**Single Skill Tested:** Select and apply the appropriate metric conversion to solve a problem embedded in a real-world NZ context
**Recommended Question Types:** NUMERIC, EXPLANATION

**Difficulty Guidance:**

| Diff | Description for This Skill | Concrete Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Single-step conversion with whole number values in a simple NZ context | A kiwifruit weighs 98 g. A tray holds 36 fruit. What is the total mass in kilograms? |
| 2 | Single-step conversion with decimal values in context | A builder needs 2.4 m of timber. The cut-list is in centimetres. How many cm does the builder need? |
| 3 | Two-step or multi-unit conversion in context | A farm water tank holds 3.5 kL. A trough holds 250 L. How many troughs can the tank fill? |
| 4 | Conversion followed by a further calculation (rate, comparison, or area) | A runner completes a 5 km race in 22 minutes 15 seconds. Express the time in seconds and calculate their average speed in m/s. |

**Example Question Stem:** A water storage tank on a farm holds 15 kL. The farmer uses 500 L per day for irrigation. How many days will the tank last?

**Common Misconceptions:**
- Student performs the arithmetic without first converting units, mixing kL and L in the same calculation
- Student converts correctly but makes an error in the subsequent arithmetic step (e.g. divides instead of multiplies when combining rates)
- Student identifies the correct units but cannot decide whether to multiply or divide in a real-world setting

## 4. Context Themes

Real-world scenarios suitable for context problems at the higher skill levels.
NZ-relevant scenarios preferred. Each theme should suggest a genuine application of the topic.

| Theme | Example Scenario |
|---|---|
| Farming and horticulture | Converting kiwifruit crop mass from grams per fruit → kg per crate → tonnes per harvest, or converting irrigation water between kL and L |
| Building and construction | Expressing timber lengths in mm for cutting plans; calculating tank volume in cm^3 and converting to litres for plumbing |
| Water storage and supply | Converting household water-tank capacity between kL and L; calculating daily usage in mL and expressing it in L |
| Sport and athletics | Converting race distances between km and m; expressing track and field times in seconds; comparing running speeds |
| Cooking and food preparation | Scaling a hāngī recipe from grams to kilograms; converting liquid ingredients between mL and L |
| Online shopping and courier delivery | Converting parcel mass between g and kg for postage rates; comparing product volumes in mL and L when choosing between sizes |
| Online gaming | Converting download file sizes or server response times; discussing storage capacity across different units |
| Public transport and travel | Calculating bus journey times in minutes and seconds; converting distance signage from km to m on a map |
