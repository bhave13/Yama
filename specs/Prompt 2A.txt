## CRITICAL RULES

Apply the following rules throughout your analysis:

**R1 — Atomisation (Boulton):**
Each skill level must test EXACTLY ONE new, independently learnable and testable skill. Never bundle two skills into a single level. If you find yourself writing "and" in a skill statement, split it into two levels.

**R2 — Ordered Progression:**
Skill levels must be ordered from most fundamental to most complex. A student must be able to succeed at Level N before attempting Level N+1.

**R3 — Flexible Level Count:**
Use as many or as few levels as the topic genuinely requires. Do not pad to fill a minimum or compress to fit a maximum. A narrow topic may have 3 levels; a rich topic may have 7 or more.

**R4 — Difficulty is Universal (1–4):**
Difficulty applies to every question at every skill level. It reflects the mathematical complexity of the values used — not the complexity of the skill itself:
- **Difficulty 1:** Integers only — whole number inputs, clean whole number answers
- **Difficulty 2:** Decimals — inputs or answers to 1–2 decimal places
- **Difficulty 3:** Fractions — exact fractional inputs or answers
- **Difficulty 4:** Mixed or complex values (e.g. surds, negative numbers, multi-step compound values)

**R5 — Question Types are Guidelines:**
Recommend question types per level but do not restrict to one. More than one type per level is acceptable. Add types not listed if pedagogically appropriate.

Available types: `NUMERIC`, `MCQ`, `MATCH`, `SPOT_ERROR/STEP`, `SPOT_ERROR/VALUE`, `EXPLANATION`

**R6 — Terminology Questions are Valid:**
At Pre-req Check and Facts levels, questions testing mathematical vocabulary — correct spelling of terms, definitions, identification of symbols — are appropriate as MCQ or MATCH questions. Example: "What is the name for the longest side of a right-angled triangle?"

**R7 — Context Problems Use Existing Types:**
Context problems wrap a real-world scenario around an existing question type. Use `NUMERIC` for calculation-in-context, `EXPLANATION` for reasoning-in-context. Do not invent a new type.

**R8 — NZ-Relevant Contexts:**
Context scenarios should be relevant to NZ students where possible: rugby fields, kiwifruit orchards, building and construction, coastal navigation, Māori design patterns, cooking and recipes, public transport.

**R9 — Year Group Determines NCEA Fields:**
The year group drives which fields are relevant in Sections 5 and 6:
- If year_group is **Year 9 or Year 10**: set `ncea_level: N/A` and `standard: N/A` in Section 6. Section 5 becomes a short Curriculum Alignment note (strand and AO only — no grade criteria table).
- If year_group is **NCEA Level 1, 2, or 3**: include the full NCEA alignment section (standard, strand, AO, and Achieved/Merit/Excellence criteria). The ncea_level value (1, 2, or 3) must appear explicitly in Section 6 as it is injected into every generated question object.
